# Governance

## Overview

Hiroba is a community-driven project under the 7KGroup organization. This document describes how the project is governed, how decisions are made, and how contributors can grow into leadership roles.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who submits a pull request, opens an issue, participates in discussions, or otherwise contributes to the project. All contributors must follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

### Maintainers

Trusted contributors with merge access and responsibility for the project's direction. Maintainers:

- Review and merge pull requests
- Triage issues and manage releases
- Participate in roadmap planning
- Mentor new contributors

### Steering Committee

A small group (3-5 members) from 7KGroup responsible for:

- High-level project direction and strategy
- Resolving disputes that maintainers cannot resolve
- Managing organization-level resources and infrastructure
- Approving new repositories under the 7KGroup organization

## Decision Making

- **Day-to-day decisions** are made by maintainers through PR reviews and issue triage
- **Significant changes** (new templates, architectural decisions, process changes) require an RFC (Request for Comments) discussed in a GitHub Discussion or Issue
- **Strategic decisions** are made by the Steering Committee with community input

## Becoming a Maintainer

Contributors who have demonstrated sustained, high-quality contributions may be nominated for maintainer status. The nomination process:

1. An existing maintainer nominates the contributor
2. The nomination is discussed among current maintainers
3. Approval requires consensus among maintainers and Steering Committee sign-off

## Changes to Governance

Changes to this governance model require approval from the Steering Committee and a public comment period of at least 14 days.
